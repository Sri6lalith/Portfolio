About Me

Hey there! I graduated with a Master's in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Northeastern University, where I specialized in Computer Networks and Security. What drives me every day is the fascinating challenge of securing our increasingly connected digital world.

My journey has been quite unique – I've got my hands dirty with everything from designing PCBs and programming microcontrollers to hunting down vulnerabilities and analyzing network traffic. This diverse background gives me a rare perspective where I can think like both a hardware engineer and a cybersecurity analyst, understanding threats from the silicon level all the way up to the application layer.

I'm particularly passionate about penetration testing and vulnerability assessment – there's something incredibly satisfying about thinking like an attacker to better defend our systems. Whether I'm setting up honeypots to catch bad actors or building mixed-reality visualizations to understand network traffic patterns, I love finding creative solutions to complex security challenges.

When I'm not breaking (and fixing) things in the lab, you'll find me staying up-to-date with the latest security trends, working on my certifications, or contributing to the cybersecurity community. I believe that in our field, continuous learning isn't just important – it's essential for staying ahead of ever-evolving threats.
